# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SEOULMATE is for Thai and Korean people who want a more trustworthy way to meet, date, make friends, and exchange language or culture. Users may arrive with romantic intent, social curiosity, or a practical language-exchange goal, but they share the same need: a space that feels safer, more respectful, and more culturally aware than casual swipe-first dating apps.

## Product Purpose

SEOULMATE helps Thai and Korean users discover compatible people, join lightweight community conversations, chat across language barriers, and build confidence before meeting or deepening a connection. Success means users feel that profiles, prompts, topics, translations, and safety checks reduce awkwardness and risk while keeping the experience warm enough to return to daily.

## Brand Personality

Modern, trustworthy, playful.

The product should feel polished and Seoul-current without becoming cold. It should carry enough formality to earn trust around dating, identity, and cross-cultural communication, while still leaving room for charm, warmth, and small moments of fun.

## Anti-references

SEOULMATE should not feel like a shallow swipe-only dating app, a scammy social network, a sugary romance game, or a corporate SaaS dashboard. Avoid manipulative urgency, oversexualized presentation, anonymous low-trust interactions, generic dating-app gradients, and visual patterns that make serious relationship intent feel disposable.

## Design Principles

1. Trust comes before excitement: safety, clarity, and respectful pacing should be visible in the interface before playful flourishes.
2. Cross-cultural by default: Thai and Korean language, tone, and expectations should feel first-class rather than translated as an afterthought.
3. Warm formality: use a composed product structure with moments of personality, not chaos or excessive cuteness.
4. Reduce first-message friction: prompts, topics, translations, and profile context should make interaction feel easier and less risky.
5. Make intent legible: friendship, dating, and exchange modes should be easy to scan so users understand what kind of connection they are entering.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, and touch targets. Support Thai and Korean text without cramped line-height or clipped glyphs. Avoid color-only status cues, respect reduced-motion preferences, and keep safety or moderation language plain enough for non-native English readers.
